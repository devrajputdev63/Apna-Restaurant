Dal-bati-churma
$20

Daal Bati Churma is a traditional Rajasthani dish from India consisting of three components - daal (lentil curry), bati (baked wheat dumplings), and churma (sweet crumbled wheat). The daal is made from a mixture of lentils, cooked with a blend of spices, while the bati is baked in a charcoal or clay oven. The churma is a sweet and crumbly dessert made by crushing the baked bati and mixing it with ghee and jaggery. It is a popular and hearty meal that is usually served during festivals or special occasions.

Tandoori-Roti
$21

Tandoori roti is a type of Indian flatbread that is cooked in a tandoor (clay oven). It is made from a mixture of whole wheat flour, water, and salt, and is rolled into a thin round shape before being baked in the tandoor. The intense heat of the oven gives the roti a slightly crispy exterior while keeping it soft and fluffy on the inside. It is often served with curries or used to wrap kebabs and other grilled meats.

Butter naan
$11

Butter naan is a type of Indian bread made from refined flour, yeast, sugar, salt, yogurt, and ghee. The dough is rolled out into a flattened shape, and then baked in a tandoor (clay oven), giving it a soft, fluffy texture with a slightly crispy exterior. The naan is brushed with melted butter while still hot, giving it a rich and buttery flavor. It is often served as an accompaniment to curries or kebabs and can be enjoyed with a variety of condiments such as raita, chutney, or pickle.

Biryani
$21

Biryani is a popular Indian and South Asian dish that consists of spiced rice cooked with meat (such as chicken, lamb, or beef), fish or vegetables, along with a variety of aromatic spices, herbs, and sometimes nuts or dried fruits. The dish is typically cooked in layers, with the rice and meat/vegetables cooked separately, and then layered together to allow the flavors to meld. Biryani is often served with raita (a yogurt-based condiment) and a side of salad or pickle. It is a flavorful and hearty dish that is enjoyed by people of all ages.

Rajma-Chawal
$23

Rajma chawal is a popular North Indian dish consisting of red kidney beans (rajma) cooked in a spicy tomato-based gravy and served with steamed white rice (chawal). The dish is made by soaking and boiling the beans until they are tender, then simmering them with a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, garam masala, and turmeric. The beans are then mixed with a tomato and onion-based sauce, and the dish is garnished with chopped coriander leaves. Rajma chawal is a comfort food for many Indians and is often served on special occasions or as a quick and easy weeknight meal.
