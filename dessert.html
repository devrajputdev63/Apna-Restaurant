Rasgulla

Rasgulla is a popular dessert from the Indian subcontinent made by boiling chenna (cottage cheese) balls in a sugar syrup. It has a spongy texture and is often served chilled, garnished with pistachios or saffron strands. Rasgulla is commonly enjoyed during festivals and celebrations, and is a favorite among sweet lovers.

Gulab Jamun is a traditional Indian dessert made by kneading milk solids (khoya) into a dough, shaping it into small balls, and deep-frying until golden brown. The fried balls are then soaked in a sugar syrup flavored with cardamom, rose water, and saffron, giving them a sweet and aromatic flavor. Gulab Jamun is often served warm and is a popular sweet served at festivals and special occasions.

Kheer
$21

Kheer, also known as rice pudding, is a sweet dish popular in India and other parts of South Asia. It is made by simmering rice, milk, sugar, and aromatic spices like cardamom, saffron, and cinnamon until the mixture thickens and the rice is cooked. It may also be garnished with nuts and raisins. Kheer is commonly served as a dessert during festivals and celebrations, or as a sweet treat on its own.

Moti Chur Ladoo is a popular Indian sweet made from fine boondi (small fried gram flour balls) that are shaped into small, bite-sized balls and then soaked in a sugar syrup. The boondi balls are flavored with cardamom and saffron, and then pressed together to form a round ball. The name "moti chur" translates to "pearl-like balls" in Hindi, referring to the small, round shape of the ladoos. Moti Chur Ladoo is commonly enjoyed during festivals and special occasions in India.

Rabdi, also known as rabri, is a rich and creamy dessert from India made by boiling milk on low heat for a long time, until it reduces to a thick and creamy consistency. The milk is then sweetened with sugar, flavored with cardamom, saffron, and nuts, and chilled before serving. Rabdi is often served with Indian sweets like jalebi, malpua, or rasgulla, and is a popular dessert during festivals and celebrations.

Malpua is a popular Indian sweet dish made from a batter of flour, semolina, and milk, which is then deep-fried until golden brown. The fried malpuas are then soaked in a sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and saffron, giving them a sweet and aromatic flavor. Malpua may also be garnished with nuts, such as pistachios or almonds, and served warm or at room temperature. It is a common dessert during festivals and special occasions in India.

Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular sweet from India and Pakistan, made by deep-frying a batter made from fermented flour and yogurt, and then soaking it in a sugar syrup flavored with saffron and rose water. Jalebis have a crispy texture on the outside and are juicy and syrupy on the inside, with a tangy and sweet flavor. They are often served hot or warm and are a common dessert during festivals and special occasions in India. Jalebis are often eaten as a snack with a cup of hot tea or milk.
